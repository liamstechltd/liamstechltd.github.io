Coping Strategies & Self-Care

🌬️ Deep Breathing Exercises

Practice the 4-7-8 technique: Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. This helps activate your body's relaxation response and reduce anxiety.

🧘 Mindfulness & Meditation

Try apps like Headspace or Calm for guided meditations. Even 5 minutes daily can help reduce stress and improve emotional regulation.

📱 Digital Detox

Set boundaries with social media and news consumption. Consider phone-free meals or designating tech-free zones in your home.

🏃 Physical Activity

Regular exercise releases endorphins. Even a 10-minute walk can boost your mood. Find activities you enjoy - dancing, swimming, or yoga.

💤 Sleep Hygiene

Maintain consistent sleep schedules, create a relaxing bedtime routine, and limit screens before bed. Quality sleep is crucial for mental health.

🤝 Connect with Others

Reach out to friends, family, or support groups. Social connection is vital for mental wellbeing - even a text to check in can help.

📝 Journaling

Write down your thoughts and feelings. Try gratitude journaling - listing 3 things you're grateful for each day can shift perspective.

🎨 Creative Expression

Art, music, writing, or crafting can be therapeutic outlets. Express yourself without judgment - the process is more important than the result.

Mental Health Emergency Guide

🚨 Immediate Danger

Call 999 immediately if:

  • You have seriously injured yourself
  • You have taken an overdose
  • You are at immediate risk of harm
  • Someone else is at immediate risk

⚠️ Crisis Situation

Contact crisis services if:

  • You're having suicidal thoughts
  • You're experiencing severe depression
  • You're having a panic attack
  • You feel unable to cope

Call Samaritans: 116 123

🏥 Getting Professional Help

Steps to take:

  • Contact your GP for a referral
  • Visit A&E if you can't wait
  • Call NHS 111 for guidance
  • Contact your local mental health team

👥 Support Network

Build your support system:

  • Identify trusted friends/family
  • Join support groups
  • Consider online communities
  • Inform your workplace if needed
